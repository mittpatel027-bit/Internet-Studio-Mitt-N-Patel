  • An example of clickable elements not being obvious is the UTD website as the image carousel with text doesn't seem to be obvious buttons.
  • A story format will allow you to truly be in the user's shoes. Understanding how someone else will navigate, and utilize your website will help you make user-informed decisions when designing the website. Additionally teams benefit greatly from a solidified story as everyone can use it as a guide for a more cohesive website.
  • Hierarchy in typography can be created in multiple ways. To name a few: Size, weight, color, style, spacing, and contrast. A good example of this is Wikipedia as each wikipedia article utilizes both size and weight to section off and organize information.

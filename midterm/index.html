About Chicago

Architectural Legacy

Chicago is a living museum of architectural innovation. From the neo-Gothic Tribune Tower to the sleek lines of the Willis Tower, the city’s skyline tells a story of ambition and reinvention. It’s the birthplace of the skyscraper and home to the influential Chicago School of architecture, making it a pilgrimage site for design lovers and urban theorists alike.

Music and Culture

Chicago hums with artistic energy. The city gave rise to electric blues and nurtured jazz legends like Louis Armstrong. Today, its neighborhoods pulse with creativity—from the murals of Pilsen to the stages of the Goodman Theatre. Whether you're catching a set at Green Mill or browsing the Art Institute, Chicago’s cultural offerings are as bold and diverse as its people.

Vote for The Lorax for President 2029

About The Lorax

The Lorax is a fictional character created by Dr. Seuss, known for his role as the passionate guardian of the environment in the 1971 book The Lorax. Small, orange, and mustachioed, the Lorax speaks for the trees and other natural elements that cannot defend themselves. He confronts the Once-ler, a figure of unchecked industrialization, warning against the consequences of exploiting nature for profit. Despite his efforts, the forest is destroyed—but the Lorax leaves behind a message of hope and responsibility: “Unless someone like you cares a whole awful lot, nothing is going to get better. It’s not.” His character embodies ecological stewardship and moral courage.

The Lorax should be president in 2029 because he represents principled leadership rooted in environmental stewardship and moral courage. He speaks for the trees and all living things that cannot speak for themselves, challenging industries and individuals to act responsibly. His presidency would prioritize clean air, renewable energy, and long-term sustainability. He would inspire citizens to care deeply about their communities and future generations. With the Lorax in office, justice for the planet would become a national priority.

Running-Mate: Zohran Mamdani

Zohran Kwame Mamdani is an American politician and member of the New York State Assembly representing the 36th district in Queens since 2021. Born on October 18, 1991, in Kampala, Uganda, he moved to New York City at age seven and later became a U.S. citizen in 2018. Mamdani is affiliated with the Democratic Party, the Democratic Socialists of America, and the Working Families Party. Before entering politics, he worked as a foreclosure prevention housing counselor, advocating for low-income homeowners in Queens. He holds a BA in Africana Studies from Bowdoin College and is the son of scholar Mahmood Mamdani and filmmaker Mira Nair

Zohran Mamdani could be seen as a compelling presidential candidate by those who value grassroots activism, economic justice, and bold progressive policies. His background as a housing counselor and his work in the New York State Assembly reflect a deep commitment to tenants' rights, healthcare access, and immigrant protections. Supporters might argue that his experience navigating complex urban issues and his affiliation with the Democratic Socialists of America show a readiness to challenge entrenched systems and prioritize people over profit. His multicultural upbringing and fluency in multiple languages also suggest a global perspective and inclusive leadership style.

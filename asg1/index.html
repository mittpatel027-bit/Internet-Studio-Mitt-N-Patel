What I Learned

  1. The web initially started as “read-only.” During the first stage of the World Wide Web, known as “Web 1.0,” the internet only had the capability to send text emails.
  2. The next generation of the web is known as “Web 3.0”, which will feature machine-to-machine interaction. It’s predicted that data will no longer be owned and instead will be shared repeatedly among multiple machines to create a sort of human-like artificial intelligence.
  3. The decline of personal websites due to centralized social media, with the additional development of Web 3.0, calls for an increase in thoughtful creation. Reliance on third-party frameworks will decrease as these applications come with performance and environmental costs.

Semester Project

I want to create a website that aids artists in finding pieces that include specific items or settings. Similar to Pinterest, but specifically only for visual art made by human hands. As an artist myself, I believe a website like this needs to exist as the practice of master studies is slowly becoming more difficult with the increase of machine-generated images. Additionally, I think one of the best ways to learn how to draw is by observing how other artists draw.
